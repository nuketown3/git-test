About

Peanut butter is a spread made from ground dry roasted peanuts. It can be creamy or crunchy and is used in sandwiches, baking, and sauces.

Uses

  • Sandwiches (PB&J)
  • Smoothies and oatmeal
  • Sauces for noodles or satay
  • Baking cookies and energy bars

Peanut Butter Jelly — The Quick Silly Sandwich

A short, funny recipe for maximum joy and minimal effort.

Ingredients

  • 2 slices of bread (the more dramatic the loaf, the better)
  • 2 tbsp peanut butter (choose your vibe: crunchy or smooth)
  • 1–2 tbsp jelly or jam (the louder the color, the louder the song)
  • Optional: a banana, a tiny drum solo, and confidence

Instructions

  1. Slap peanut butter on one slice. Slap jelly on the other. Try not to dance yet.
  2. Press slices together with enthusiasm. If you hear "Peanut Butter Jelly Time" in your head, that's normal.
  3. Optional: cut into funky shapes, perform a victory dance, and enjoy.

Nutrition (per 2 tbsp)

Approximate values:

  • Calories: 180–200
  • Protein: 7–8 g
  • Fat: 16 g
  • Carbs: 6–8 g

Notes

Choose natural peanut butter without added sugars for a cleaner ingredient list. Store unopened jars in a cool, dry place; refrigerate after opening for longer freshness.
